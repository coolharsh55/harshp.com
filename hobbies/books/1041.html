  • review: This was supposed to be old, as in really old WW era, science fiction. And while it was certainly slow, what came across was a story. Something that preceded many other ideas. A clone of your loved one who doesn't love you back, and what might that clone then do -- won't it love you? A simple premise that plays out as a social drama. Yes, there are elements of science fiction still in there, nearly half a century past. The sexism of the times does come across in places, sometimes strongly, such as when referring to asking permission from 'him' about 'her', but there is not a lot of it beyond the phrasing. I picked up a cheap copy - hardly 3 euros courtesy of the British Library. So this was a good exercercise in reading some older literature with a still interesting story.,
