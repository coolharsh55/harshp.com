  • review: The basic premise is that Mars has a colony that is a stand-in for China, and Earth for western ethos. Amidst this, we get some cultural exchange discussions which are supposed to discuss the egalitarian and moral values of both sides, their contrasts, and the necessities as justified by each, while also being biased to continue propogandeering their own side for continued existence. Intriguing in theory. But the book itself, which was quite long - twice as much, was filled with too much information, not an easy flowing story, and sometimes felt like a textbook with the characters being so flat and difficult to keep up with. Important events happen but we get no characterisations but the amount of information about cultural associations in even minor lessons is given several pages. This was a difficult read. Reminded me of Robinson and Le Guin in some of their works, which felt at least like they knew what they were after and you could feel the story take you there.,
